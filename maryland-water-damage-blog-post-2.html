Under Maryland law, insurance companies cannot:

Additional Living Expenses (ALE)

Maryland policies typically include ALE coverage while your home is uninhabitable:

  • Hotel/Temporary Housing: Reasonable accommodation costs
  • Meal Expenses: Additional costs above normal food budget
  • Storage Fees: Protecting belongings during restoration
  • Pet Boarding: If temporary housing doesn't allow pets
  • Extended Commute: Additional transportation costs

Personal Property Claims

Document and claim all water-damaged personal property:

  • Electronics: TVs, computers, appliances (even if they seem to work)
  • Furniture: Wood furniture absorbs water and may warp later
  • Clothing: Items exposed to contaminated water
  • Documents: Professional document restoration services
  • Sentimental Items: Photos, artwork, family heirlooms

Hidden Damage Claims

Your expert should identify and claim damage not immediately visible:

  • Behind-Wall Moisture: Insulation and framing damage
  • HVAC System Contamination: Ductwork cleaning or replacement
  • Electrical System Exposure: Outlets, wiring, and panels
  • Subflooring Damage: Moisture beneath flooring materials
